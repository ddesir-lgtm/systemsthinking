Systems perspective

Rules, goals, and adaptation in the regional labor system

This project treats SB 1718 not as a one-time shock, but as a change in the rules of the labor system. The law raises the legal cost of direct undocumented employment without changing the underlying demand for labor in construction and hospitality. The system therefore adapts rather than contracts. Firms shift toward alternative labor channels, reorganize across more establishments, and preserve visible employment. The central intervention is not just “better data.” It is changing the rules conversation and the system goal being evaluated.

System problemThe policy targets a category of workers rather than the underlying demand for labor.
Observed adaptationH-2B demand rises, establishment structure changes, and formal hiring intensity falls.
Primary leverage pointChallenge the rules and goals that define policy “success,” not just the measurement of outcomes.
Intervention logicReframe the issue from worker legality to employer demand, substitution, and accountability.
Interpretive frame

What the project is trying to change

The strongest contribution of the project is to show that targeting undocumented labor alone does not reduce reliance on immigrant labor. Instead, it changes how that reliance is organized. That means the strategic move for UnidosNow is to intervene at the level of rules (how employers can adapt without accountability) and system goals (whether policy success is defined as removing undocumented workers from visible payrolls, or reducing exploitative dependence on immigrant labor overall).
